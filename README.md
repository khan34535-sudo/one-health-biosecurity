A privacy-first, zero-knowledge biosecurity surveillance framework engineered for offline edge computing. This application integrates human clinical anomalies, zoonotic livestock/sylvatic vectors, and environmental metadata to execute decentralized risk stratification under the **WHO One Health BERM v2.0 protocol**.

Traditional global health monitoring relies on centralized cloud networks that scrape Personally Identifiable Information (PII), creating massive international legal bottlenecks, data-sovereignty battles, and systemic reporting delays. **One Health Biosecurity** bypasses this paradigm entirely by shifting the mathematical engine 100% client-side, running locally within any standard modern web browser DOM.

---

## 🌟 Key Features

* **One Health Triad Integration:** Evaluates overlapping risks across human clinical systems, avian/mammalian zoonotic vectors (canine, feline, livestock, and rodent interfaces), and environmental dynamics (geographical terrain, vector habitats, and toxicological layers).
* **Zero-Knowledge (ZK) Local Architecture:** Zero data collection. No cloud databases, no trackers, and no remote storage. Every statistical calculation occurs natively on the user's local device, rendering the tool inherently compliant with global data privacy frameworks (such as GDPR and HIPAA).
* **Discontinuous Offline Update Model:** Designed for deep field operations, border checkpoints, and isolated clinics. The framework can update its global epidemiological matrices completely offline via **Inbound Master Trend QR Codes** or close-range peer-to-peer mesh syncing.
* **Cryptographic Triage Handovers:** Generates compressed, PII-free **Outbound QR Tokens** right on the screen. These tokens allow field workers to securely hand over anonymized risk-stratification metrics to external healthcare networks or border authorities without executing individual human surveillance.

## 🗺️ Global Surveillance Matrix Coverage

The framework contains specialized localized risk profiles across critical global hubs, tracking endemic variations such as:
* **Middle East (Oman / Saudi Arabia):** Marine and desert toxicological vectors (*Echis omanensis*), Alkhurma Hemorrhagic Fever, and camel-to-human MERS-CoV dynamics.
* **South Asia (Pakistan / India):** XDR Typhoid profiles, Crimean-Congo Hemorrhagic Fever livestock layers, "Big Four" clinical envenomation matrices, and *Nipah virus* fruit bat vectors.
* **South America (Brazil):** Endemic Dengue serotypes and zoonotic feline-to-human *Sporothrix brasiliensis* fungal transmission layers.
* **West Africa (Nigeria):** Seasonal Lassa Fever (*Mastomys natalensis* rodent paths) and urban stray canine rabies dynamics.
* **Western Europe (United Kingdom):** Historical Bovine Spongiform Encephalopathy (BSE/Mad Cow Disease) environmental exposure layers for variant Creutzfeldt-Jakob Disease (vCJD) tracking.

## 🛠️ Tech Stack & Architecture

* **Frontend Architecture:** Single-file architecture utilizing structural HTML5, vanilla CSS3 grid layouts, and responsive UI design.
* **Algorithmic Engine:** Client-side JavaScript (`performSearch()` pipelines, responsive coordinate layer maps, and local mathematical triage mapping).
* **Deployment Format:** Designed as a lightweight Progressive Web App (PWA) for one-time download and permanent offline mobile execution.
